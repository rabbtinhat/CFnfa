Crypto Market Indicators

Daily BTC Whale vs. Retail Net Buying/Selling Volume

Source: BGeometrics
×

Tracks the net daily buying/selling volume of Bitcoin by whales and retail investors. Whale activity is often seen as a market indicator due to their capital influence on prices, while retail behavior reflects broader market sentiment.

How to Use

  • Track Whale Movements: Sustained net buying by whales may signal bullish expectations; heavy selling could indicate short-term peaks or risks.
  • Retail Behavior Analysis: Concentrated retail buying often coincides with market overheating (e.g., FOMO), potentially leading to corrections. Panic selling by retail may signal a bottom.

Pros

  • Captures real-time sentiment via capital flows

Cons

  • Data lag; whales may hide actions across multiple accounts

BTC MVRV Chart (Market Value to Realized Value)

Source: CoinGlass
×

MVRV compares Bitcoin's market cap to its realized cap, assessing price deviations from "fair value."

How to Use

  • Overbought/Oversold: MVRV > 3.5 suggests overvaluation; MVRV < 1 implies undervaluation
  • Trend Reversals: A drop from extreme highs signals bull-to-bear shifts

Pros

  • Effective for gauging market profitability via on-chain data

Cons

  • Fails to predict black swan events; historical ranges may shift

BTC Rainbow Chart

Source: CoinGlass
×

Colored bands show BTC's cyclical price position: warm colors (red) = overvalued, cool colors (blue) = undervalued.

How to Use

  • Cycle Timing: Buy in deep blue bands; sell in red bands
  • Sentiment Alignment: Colors correlate with greed/fear

Pros

  • Visually intuitive for market phases

Cons

  • Relies solely on price history; ignores fundamentals

BTC Spot ETF Inflows

Source: Farside Investors
×

Tracks net inflows/outflows of U.S.-approved Bitcoin spot ETFs

How to Use

  • Momentum: Sustained inflows suggest institutional accumulation
  • Confidence Gauge: ETF flows indicate traditional finance adoption

Pros

  • Direct insight into Wall Street capital

Cons

  • Short-term price decoupling (e.g., arbitrage trades)

Liquidation Heatmap

Source: CoinGlass
×

Visualizes forced liquidation volumes across price levels in futures markets

How to Use

  • Key Levels: High liquidation clusters may trigger volatility
  • Long/Short Signals: Upper heat = short squeeze risk; lower = long squeeze risk

Pros

  • Identifies leverage-driven vulnerabilities

Cons

  • Manipulation risks (e.g., "stop hunts")

Fear & Greed Index

Source: CoinGlass
×

Scores market sentiment (0-100) using volatility, volume, social trends

How to Use

  • Contrarian Signal: >75 = greed (caution); <25 = fear (opportunity)
  • Divergence Alert: Rising prices + flat index = weak rally

Pros

  • Holistic sentiment gauge

Cons

  • Opaque methodology; extremes can persist

Stablecoin Market Cap

Source: CoinGlass
×

Tracks total USD-pegged stablecoin supply (e.g., USDT, USDC), reflecting crypto's "dry powder."

How to Use

  • Bullish Signal: Rising cap = potential buying pressure.
  • Liquidity Risk: Sharp drops may trigger sell-offs (e.g., UST collapse).

Pros

  • Direct liquidity measure.

Cons

  • Exclude operational factors (e.g., Tether minting).
