Crypto Market Metrics

1. Daily BTC Whale vs. Retail Net Buying/Selling Volume

Source: BGeometrics
×

Tracks the net daily buying/selling volume of Bitcoin by whales and retail investors. Whale activity is often seen as a market indicator due to their capital influence on prices, while retail behavior reflects broader market sentiment.

How to Use

  • Track Whale Movements: Sustained net buying by whales may signal bullish expectations; heavy selling could indicate short-term peaks or risks.
  • Retail Behavior Analysis: Concentrated retail buying often coincides with market overheating (e.g., FOMO), potentially leading to corrections. Panic selling by retail may signal a bottom.

Pros

  • Captures real-time sentiment via capital flows

Cons

  • Data lag; whales may hide actions across multiple accounts

2. BTC MVRV Chart

Graph Placeholder
Source: CoinGlass
×

MVRV compares Bitcoin's market cap to its realized cap (calculated based on the last moved price of each BTC), assessing whether prices deviate from "fair value."

How to Use

  • Overbought/Oversold: MVRV > 3.5 suggests overvaluation; MVRV < 1 implies undervaluation.
  • Trend Reversals: A drop from extreme highs signals bull-to-bear shifts; a rebound from lows may indicate a bottom.

Pros

  • Effective for gauging market profitability via on-chain data

Cons

  • Fails to predict black swan events; historical ranges may shift

3. BTC Rainbow Chart

Graph Placeholder
Source: CoinGlass
×

This chart uses colored bands to show BTC's cyclical price position: warm colors (red) = overvalued, cool colors (blue) = undervalued.

How to Use

  • Cycle Timing: Buy in deep blue bands (long-term opportunities); sell in red bands.
  • Sentiment Alignment: Colors correlate with greed/fear, aiding contrarian strategies.

Pros

  • Visually intuitive for market phases

Cons

  • Relies solely on price history; ignores fundamentals

Source: Farside Investors

Tracks net inflows/outflows of U.S.-approved Bitcoin spot ETFs, reflecting institutional and retail capital trends.

How to Use

  • Momentum: Sustained inflows suggest institutional accumulation (bullish); outflows signal caution.
  • Confidence Gauge: ETF flows correlate with traditional finance adoption (long-term trend indicator).

Pros

  • Direct insight into Wall Street capital

Cons

  • Short-term price decoupling (e.g., arbitrage trades)
