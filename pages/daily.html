North America Market

U.S. equity indices declined Tuesday, with the S&P 500 down 1.1% and Nasdaq dropping 1.7% amid tech sector weakness. Investors awaited the Federal Reserve’s policy decision, expecting rates to hold steady but focused on updated economic projections and tariff-related risks. The “Magnificent Seven” tech stocks underperformed, dragging indexes lower, while Tesla fell 5% due to Chinese EV competition. Bank of America’s survey highlighted a historic drop in bullish sentiment, citing tariff fears and growth concerns. Treasury yields edged lower, with the 10-year at 4.287% as markets priced in potential rate cuts later this year.

Europe Market

Germany’s Bundestag passed a historic debt reform, suspending fiscal constraints to boost defense spending and a €500B climate fund. The DAX rose 0.3% on the news, though broader European markets were mixed amid U.S. tariff uncertainty. The OECD cut its 2025 U.S. growth forecast to 2.2%, citing trade risks. Auto stocks gained as Zeekr announced free autonomous driving software in China, while energy firms rallied on Trump’s coal production push. The Stoxx 600 rose 0.8%, supported by industrials, though defensive sectors lagged as investors awaited Fed guidance and tariff clarity.

Asia Market

Asian markets faced pressure from U.S. tech declines and tariff concerns, with Hong Kong’s Hang Seng down 1.2%. Chinese EV makers BYD and Xiaomi surged on battery tech updates and production hikes, contrasting with Tesla’s 53% drop from December highs. Japan’s Nikkei slid 0.7% as exporters weighed yen volatility. XPeng rose 4% after strong Q1 guidance, while South Korean chip stocks mirrored Nvidia’s 3% drop. Investors monitored U.S.-China trade tensions, particularly potential retaliatory measures against Trump’s tariffs, which could further disrupt regional supply chains and export-dependent economies.

Technology Sector

Tech stocks led declines as the “Magnificent Seven” posted their worst quarter since 2022. Nvidia fell 3% despite unveiling new AI chips at its GTC event, while Alphabet dropped 2.7% after acquiring cybersecurity firm Wiz for $32B. Tesla slid 5% amid BYD’s 1,000kW charging tech launch and Xiaomi’s expanded EV production. Meta turned negative YTD, and Apple lost 18% from December highs. AI-related concerns persisted, with Goldman Sachs noting tech’s “structural growth” remains intact but warning of near-term volatility. Semiconductor stocks like Super Micro Computer plunged 9.6% on growth fears.

Stablecoins (USDT, USDC, DAI) held parity, reflecting risk-off sentiment. Total trading volume remained elevated at $238B+ for BTC and $96B+ for ETH, suggesting heightened activity despite price drops. 2) **Key movers**: TRON (+6.71%) and Bitget Token (+4.01%) outperformed, with TRX volume up 255% vs. its 7d average. Toncoin extended its weekly rally (+33.06%) despite modest 24h gains (+2.17%). Dogecoin (-4.90%), Shiba Inu (-4.90%), and Litecoin (-5.27%) underperformed sharply. Pi Network collapsed (-16.53%) on high volume, accelerating its 7d decline (-20.68%). 3) **Dominant trends**: Mid-caps showed divergence: BNB (+13.08% weekly) and MANTRA (+10.05%) resisted bearish pressure, while ADA (-5.01% weekly) lagged. Sector rotation appeared toward protocols with strong utility narratives (TON’s ecosystem growth, BNB’s chain activity). Large-cap dominance dipped slightly (BTC: 60.52%, ETH: 8.57%) as traders rebalanced into select mid-caps. Bearish momentum dominated short-term action, though Toncoin’s sustained rally and stablecoin inflows hinted at selective accumulation.
