North America Market

U.S. equities rebounded sharply after President Trump delayed auto tariffs on Canada and Mexico by one month, easing trade war fears. The S&P 500 (+1.1%), Dow (+1.1%), and Nasdaq (+1.4%) surged, led by automakers Ford (+5.8%), GM (+7.2%), and Stellantis (+9.2%). Weak ADP jobs data (77K vs. expected 150K) initially pressured markets, but tariff relief overshadowed concerns. Industrials and materials sectors outperformed amid a weaker dollar. Disney cut 200 jobs in its ABC News division, while Mars Inc. issued a record $26B bond to fund its Kellanova acquisition. Defense stocks like Huntington Ingalls (+12.3%) rallied on Trump’s shipbuilding focus.

Europe Market

European markets rallied as Germany announced plans to boost defense and infrastructure spending, fueling a 1.5% euro surge to $1.0789 (3-day high). The Stoxx 600 rose, led by industrials and automotive stocks recovering from tariff-driven losses. BlackRock’s $22.8B Panama Canal port deal aligned with Trump’s trade agenda, lifting infrastructure-linked equities. However, auto tariffs and retaliatory risks lingered, with EU leaders monitoring U.S. policy shifts. The euro’s rally sparked bullish options bets targeting $1.20, though analysts debated sustainability. UK’s FTSE underperformed amid sterling strength and energy sector drags from falling oil prices.

Asia Market

Asian markets were mixed: China’s CSI 300 (+0.4%) rose on Beijing’s 5% GDP target and tech support vows, while Japan’s Nikkei (-0.8%) dipped on yen strength. South Korean K-pop stocks (HYBE, JYP) outperformed, immune to U.S. tariff risks. Emerging markets gained as the dollar weakened, with MSCI’s EM index up 2.1%. India’s Sensex edged higher on domestic growth optimism, though Taiwan and Hong Kong traded cautiously amid tech supply chain concerns. Australia’s ASX 200 (-0.8%) fell despite Q4 GDP beat, weighed by mining stocks on tariff-linked demand fears.

Technology Sector

Tech stocks rebounded with Nasdaq (+1.5%), though CrowdStrike (-6.3%) sank on weak guidance. Moderna (+15.9%) surged on CEO stock buys and cancer vaccine updates. Cybersecurity firms like Zscaler (+4%) rose post-earnings, while MongoDB (-18%) plunged on soft outlook. AI-linked chip stocks (Nvidia +2%) recovered partially from tariff-driven losses. Elon Musk’s DOGE workforce cuts threatened D.C.-area restaurants (Cava, Sweetgreen). Disney’s streaming unit avoided layoffs, but tech consolidation continued. AppLovin (+2%) gained on gaming unit sale talks, while Arista Networks (+34% target) was upgraded by UBS on cloud demand.
