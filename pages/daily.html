North America Market

U.S. markets rebounded cautiously, led by megacap tech stocks, with the Nasdaq up 1.2% and S&P 500 gaining 0.5%, while the Dow dipped 0.2%. Nvidia (+6%) and Tesla (+7%) surged, buoyed by moderating core CPI (3.1% YoY, lowest since 2021). However, trade tensions persisted as Canada/EU retaliated against U.S. tariffs. Intel jumped 11% post-market on CEO Lip-Bu Tan’s appointment and reports of a TSMC-led consortium eyeing its manufacturing unit. JPMorgan slashed Tesla’s price target amid delivery concerns, though shares rallied with broader tech.

Europe Market

European markets faced pressure amid U.S. tariff uncertainty and recession fears, though specific indices were less covered. Trade war risks dominated sentiment, with EU counter-tariffs on $28B U.S. goods adding to volatility. Defensive sectors like healthcare underperformed as investors rotated into beaten-down tech. Inflation concerns lingered despite softer U.S. CPI, with focus on potential spillover from tariffs. Automakers saw mixed reactions; Nissan rose slightly on CEO transition news, while broader industrials remained cautious amid supply chain risks.

Asia Market

Asia-Pacific markets traded mixed: Japan’s Nikkei flat, South Korea’s Kospi up 1.5%, while Hong Kong’s Hang Seng fell 1.4%. India’s inflation eased to 3.98% (Feb), boosting sentiment. Nissan (+0.6%) rose on CEO change, though Honda dipped. China’s 10-year bond yield neared 2%, reflecting growth concerns. U.S. tariff threats and weaker Tesla sales in Europe/Asia weighed on regional automakers. Japan’s wholesale inflation slowed to 4%, keeping BOJ rate hikes in focus. Indian equities lagged despite strong IPO momentum, with Nifty down 0.6%.

Technology Sector

Intel surged 11% after naming Lip-Bu Tan CEO and reports of a TSMC-Nvidia-Broadcom consortium eyeing its manufacturing arm. Tesla gained 7.6% despite U.S./global sales declines and JPMorgan’s bearish delivery forecast. Microsoft dipped briefly on FTC antitrust probe updates. Marvell Tech plunged (-40% YTD) despite data center AI growth, citing weak non-AI segments. Adobe fell 3% on soft guidance. Broader tech rallied on cooling CPI, though trade risks persisted. Morgan Stanley highlighted Tesla’s AI/autonomy potential, while Evercore cut delivery estimates amid brand erosion tied to Musk’s political activities.

Crypto

Bitcoin (+4.3%) and Ethereum (+2.1%) led gains among majors, while stablecoins USDT and USDC held steady. Bitcoin’s market cap surpassed $1.64T, reflecting renewed institutional interest. Mid-cap assets like Cardano (+6.7%) and Bitcoin Cash (+2.2%) mirrored this rebound. Volatility persisted as traders balanced technical retracements against macroeconomic uncertainty, with stablecoin volumes signaling cautious capital rotation.
