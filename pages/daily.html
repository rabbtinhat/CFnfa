North America Market

U.S. markets closed mixed ahead of Good Friday, with the Dow dropping 1.3% (led by UnitedHealth’s 22% plunge on weak guidance), while the S&P 500 edged up 0.1%. Netflix surged 9% post-earnings on revenue and subscriber growth optimism. Tech stocks faced pressure: Nvidia fell 3% after U.S. chip export bans to China, while AMD and Intel declined. Trade tensions persisted as Trump criticized Fed Chair Powell’s tariff warnings, sparking volatility. UnitedHealth’s $120B market cap loss dragged healthcare stocks lower. Investors weighed strong corporate earnings (e.g., Eli Lilly +14%) against macroeconomic risks from tariffs and Fed policy uncertainty.

Europe Market

European markets were closed for Good Friday, but pre-holiday trading reflected caution amid U.S. trade policy risks. Earlier in the week, concerns over Trump’s tariffs and Fed Chair Powell’s warnings about inflation and growth headwinds weighed on sentiment. Netflix’s price hike in France highlighted regional pricing adjustments. Banks and exporters remained sensitive to tariff-related volatility, while energy stocks benefited from rising oil prices. Investors await Monday’s reopening, with attention on global trade developments and earnings from European firms.

Asia Market

Asian markets faced pressure from U.S. tech export restrictions, with TSMC shares fluctuating despite strong earnings (+3% pre-Good Friday). Nvidia’s China-related revenue risks ($10B-$15B estimated loss) amplified sector concerns. Japan and South Korea’s tech-heavy indices underperformed, while Chinese equities stabilized amid tariff uncertainty. TSMC maintained its 2025 revenue outlook but flagged risks from U.S. trade policies. Regional markets will reopen Monday, with focus on U.S.-China trade talks, chip-sector developments, and broader risk sentiment driven by Trump’s tariff threats and Fed policy signals.

Technology Sector

Netflix rose 9% after beating Q1 earnings, guiding higher Q2 revenue, and emphasizing ad-tier growth. Nvidia fell 3% as U.S. export bans to China threatened $10B+ in annual sales; AMD and Intel dipped. TSMC gained 3% pre-holiday despite tariff risks, citing robust AI demand. Alphabet slid 1.4% after a U.S. court ruled it monopolized ad tech. Eli Lilly surged 14% on positive weight-loss pill trial results. Tech sector volatility persisted amid Trump’s tariff threats, Fed policy concerns, and mixed earnings, with investors balancing AI optimism against trade-related headwinds.
