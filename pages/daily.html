North America Market

U.S. equities rallied Friday, capping a volatile week driven by Trump’s tariff shifts. The S&P 500 (+1.8%), Nasdaq (+2.1%), and Dow (+1.5%) posted their best weekly gains since 2022-2023, led by tech, industrials, and financials. Bond yields surged (10-year at 4.5%), gold hit a record ($3,200/oz), and the dollar weakened. JPMorgan and Wells Fargo reported mixed earnings, with CEO Jamie Dimon warning of economic “turbulence.” Consumer sentiment fell to 2022 lows amid inflation fears. China’s retaliatory 125% tariff on U.S. goods intensified trade war concerns, though markets stabilized late Friday on hopes for negotiations.

Europe Market

European markets rebounded sharply, with the Stoxx 600 rising 3.7% (best since 2021) after the EU paused retaliatory tariffs for 90 days. Germany’s DAX surged 4.67% on industrial optimism. However, analysts cautioned EU-China alignment remains unlikely due to trade disputes and geopolitical tensions. Automakers and exporters faced uncertainty as Trump’s tariffs raised risks of diverted Chinese exports to Europe. The euro strengthened to a three-year high against the dollar, reflecting shifting capital flows. Policymakers emphasized defensive trade measures to prevent dumping but avoided escalating a two-front trade war with the U.S. and China.

Asia Market

Asian markets were mixed: Hang Seng (+1.8%) and CSI 300 (+0.5%) rose on stimulus hopes, while Nikkei (-3.4%) and Kospi (-0.7%) fell on U.S. trade risks. China hiked tariffs on U.S. goods to 125%, retaliating against Trump’s 145% levy. Tesla removed Model S/X orders in China due to tariffs, though domestic EV sales rebounded. The yuan weakened to 7.42/USD, raising devaluation concerns. Japan’s bond yields mirrored U.S. volatility, while gold and haven assets gained regionally. Exporters accelerated diversification from China-U.S. trade routes, with Vietnam and Taiwan benefiting from supply chain shifts.

Technology Sector

Tech stocks led gains: Nvidia (+3.1%) and TSMC (+4%) rose as China exempted foreign-made chips from tariffs. Tesla cut Cybertruck prices in the U.S. amid slowing demand but faced China tariff headwinds. Apple fell 4% weekly on China production risks but rebounded 5% Friday. Netflix and Amazon gained on defensive demand. U.S. chipmakers Texas Instruments (-5.7%) and Monolithic Power (+10%) diverged on tariff exemptions. Goldman Sachs upgraded Huntington Ingalls (+7.4%) on defense spending. AI and cloud infrastructure firms stabilized amid bond market turmoil, though analysts warned of earnings guidance cuts due to trade uncertainty.
