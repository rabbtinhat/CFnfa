North America Market

U.S. equities closed mixed amid heightened volatility, with the S&P 500 recovering losses (+0.6%) while the Nasdaq (-0.1%) and Dow (+1%) diverged. Markets faced their worst quarterly performance since 2022, driven by Trump’s escalating tariff threats targeting "all countries" effective April 2. Energy stocks surged as oil prices erased 2025 losses (WTI +3%, Brent +2%) on supply concerns. CVS Health (+50% YTD) led gains, while Tesla (-35%) and crypto-linked stocks (Coinbase -31%) underperformed. Consumer staples (Walmart, Costco) attracted defensive inflows amid recession fears, with hedge funds selling tech at the fastest pace in six months.

Europe Market

European markets saw muted trading with sector-specific moves. Aston Martin surged 13% after securing funding from Chairman Lawrence Stroll, though broader auto stocks faced pressure from U.S. tariff risks. The Stoxx Europe 600 remained range-bound as investors weighed potential retaliatory EU measures against U.S. tariffs. Luxury and export-heavy sectors (e.g., German automakers) lagged on trade-war anxieties, while defensive sectors like utilities held steady. The FTSE 100 underperformed due to energy volatility and GBP strength.

Asia Market

Asian markets were mixed, with China’s Shanghai Composite dragged by tech and EV weakness (Tesla sales -50% YoY in China). Japan’s Nikkei edged higher on yen depreciation, though automakers (Toyota, Honda) dipped on U.S. tariff risks. Indian indices outperformed on domestic growth optimism. Regional sentiment remained cautious ahead of U.S. tariff details, with semiconductor and export-reliant stocks (Samsung, TSMC) volatile. Bitcoin’s decline (-10% Q1) pressured crypto-linked equities in South Korea and Japan.

Technology Sector

The "Magnificent Seven" tech giants (Apple, Nvidia, Tesla) fell sharply, with Roundhill’s ETF down 15% YTD. Tesla (-35%) faced slowing sales, Musk’s political backlash, and tariff risks. Nvidia (-20%) neared bear-market territory amid AI investment doubts. Crypto stocks (Coinbase, Galaxy Digital) plunged as Bitcoin slumped. Palantir and AI-related firms weakened on Chinese competition (DeepSeek’s efficient models). Defensive rotations hit growth stocks, while semiconductor firms (KLA Corp) gained upgrades on process-control demand. Tech volatility dominated sector performance amid macro uncertainty.
