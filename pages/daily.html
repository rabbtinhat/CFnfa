North America Market

North American markets declined sharply, led by tech stocks, as President Trump’s tariffs and Fed Chair Powell’s warnings on stagflation risks fueled volatility. The S&P 500 fell 2.2%, Dow dropped 1.7%, and Nasdaq slid 3%. Nvidia and AMD plunged 7% each after disclosing $5.5B and $800M charges from U.S. chip export curbs to China. Apple lost 4%, retreating below a $3T market cap amid tariff uncertainty. Treasuries rallied as Powell signaled patience on rate cuts. Hertz surged 56% after Bill Ackman’s Pershing Square disclosed a 19.8% stake.

Europe Market

European markets faced pressure amid tariff uncertainty and growth concerns. The ECB is expected to cut rates Thursday, with traders pricing a 94% chance of a 25-bp reduction, as trade tensions threaten eurozone growth. ASML sank 7% after missing order estimates and warning of demand uncertainty due to tariffs. Lyft’s $199M acquisition of Free Now marked its European expansion. Automakers like Nissan flagged challenges relocating production to avoid U.S. tariffs. The Stoxx 600 traded lower, with tech and industrials underperforming as investors weighed recession risks from prolonged trade disputes.

Asia Market

Asian markets grappled with U.S.-China trade tensions, particularly semiconductor restrictions. Nvidia’s $5.5B charge and AMD’s $800M hit highlighted risks for tech supply chains reliant on Chinese demand. Huawei is poised to benefit from Nvidia’s H20 chip ban in China. Japan’s auto sector remained in focus as Trump’s tariffs threatened exports of 1M+ vehicles annually. South Korean chipmakers faced indirect pressure from U.S. export curbs. Regional indices traded mixed, with Chinese stocks muted amid lingering trade retaliation risks, while Japan’s Nikkei stabilized ahead of U.S.-Japan tariff talks.

Technology Sector

Tech stocks led declines as Nvidia and AMD tumbled 7% on U.S. chip export restrictions to China, with Nvidia taking a $5.5B charge. Apple fell 4% despite temporary tariff exemptions, reflecting concerns over prolonged trade tensions. ASML dropped 7% on weak orders and tariff-driven demand uncertainty. Netflix options signaled an 8.5% earnings move, with analysts bullish on its ad-tier growth. Meta and Microsoft were flagged as potential buys amid the selloff. Oracle and Broadcom slid 2-3% on semiconductor sector weakness. U.S.-China AI competition intensified, with Huawei gaining ground in chip development.
