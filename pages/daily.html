North America Market

U.S. equities plummeted Monday, with the S&P 500 down 1.7% (worst day of 2025) and Nasdaq dropping 2.6%, driven by President Trump’s confirmation of 25% tariffs on Canada and Mexico starting March 4. The Dow fell 650 points, led by tech sell-offs (Nvidia -8.7%). Tariff fears overshadowed weak economic data, including slowing consumer spending. Energy stocks also declined as OPEC+ plans to boost oil output. Cryptocurrencies pared gains after Trump’s strategic reserve announcement, with Bitcoin retreating from $95k to $86k. Investors await Friday’s jobs report and retail earnings (Target, Costco) for economic clarity.

Europe Market

European equities rose Monday, contrasting U.S. declines, amid hopes for a Ukraine peace deal and defense spending plans. The euro rebounded 0.9% against the dollar, supported by ECB rate-cut expectations and Eurozone inflation easing to 2.4%. Energy stocks underperformed as oil slid. Germany’s potential defense and infrastructure funds bolstered sentiment. The STOXX 600 gained 1.2%, led by industrials and financials. Investors focused on Thursday’s ECB meeting for policy cues. U.K. markets were buoyed by King Charles III-backed sustainable agriculture initiatives, though broader EU growth concerns lingered amid tariff-related trade risks.

Asia Market

Asia-Pacific markets were mixed: Hong Kong’s Hang Seng rose 0.5% as Mixue’s IPO surged 43% on debut, while mainland China’s CSI 300 fell 1.2% amid EV weakness (Nio -8.6%, Li Auto -10.9%). Japan’s Nikkei gained 0.8% as M&A activity boosted small-caps. India’s Nifty 50 slid 0.6% on tariff retaliation fears. South Korea’s KOSPI dropped 1.1% amid tech sector pressure. Cryptocurrency-linked stocks (Samsung, Coinbase Asia) fluctuated post-Trump’s crypto reserve update. Investors monitored U.S.-China trade tensions and Japan’s corporate reforms driving small-cap outperformance.

Technology Sector

Tech led declines: Nvidia sank 8.7% on reports of AI chips reaching China via third parties, sparking regulatory probes. Dell (-7%) and Super Micro (-13%) also fell. Tesla dropped 2% despite Morgan Stanley’s $430 price target, citing AI/robotics potential. Broadcom (-6%) and Intel (+3.5% early) fluctuated on chip partnership rumors. AppLovin surged 3.6% after a $500M buyback plan. Autonomous trucking firm Aurora Innovation rose 3% premarket on Morgan Stanley’s bullish initiation. Crypto stocks (Coinbase, MicroStrategy) pared gains post-Trump’s weekend crypto reserve announcement. AI chip earnings (Broadcom, Marvell) eyed this week.
