North America Market

North American equities faced significant pressure, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite posting their worst weekly performances since September, down over 3% and 3.6%, respectively. The Nasdaq entered correction territory, sliding 10% from December’s peak, driven by tariff uncertainty, inflation concerns, and weaker economic data. Investors await February CPI and PPI reports for clues on price trends. Analysts highlighted market volatility as a potential buying opportunity, particularly in tech and financials, though caution persists amid Fed rate-cut delays. Treasury yields stabilized, while sectors like energy and defensives saw relative resilience.

Europe Market

European markets mirrored U.S. declines, with the STOXX 600 dropping 2.3% weekly amid tariff fears and slowing growth concerns. Export-heavy sectors like autos and industrials underperformed, while defensives such as utilities lagged less. ECB policymakers echoed Fed caution, emphasizing data dependence amid sticky inflation. UK’s FTSE 100 fell 1.8%, pressured by commodity-linked stocks as metal prices retreated. European banks faced headwinds from lowered GDP forecasts, though mergers and deregulation hopes provided partial support. Investors monitored geopolitical tensions and tariff spillover risks, with volatility expected ahead of U.S. inflation data.

Asia Market

Asian markets slumped, with China’s Shanghai Composite down 4.1% weekly on tariff-related export risks and property sector woes. Japan’s Nikkei 225 fell 3.7% as tech stocks mirrored Nasdaq’s correction, while a stronger yen weighed on exporters. South Korea’s KOSPI dropped 2.9%, led by semiconductor giants Samsung and SK Hynix. India’s Nifty 50 slid 2.2% amid foreign outflows and growth fears. Regional central banks maintained cautious stances, with Australia’s ASX 200 declining 1.8% as commodity prices softened. Rising U.S. bond yields and dollar strength pressured emerging-market currencies, amplifying equity volatility.
