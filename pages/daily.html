North America Market

North American stock markets are closed due to the Presidents' Day holiday in the United States and Family Day in Canada. Before the holiday, US markets were mixed. Europe Market

European shares reached record levels, driven by defense stocks, as leaders called for an emergency summit on the Ukraine conflict. The pan-European STOXX 600 index closed at an all-time high of 555.65, rising 0.59% The optimism in European markets was fueled by hopes of peace in Ukraine, which has been a significant geopolitical concern affecting market stability. However, there are also concerns about potential tariffs and trade tensions.

Asia Market

Overall, Asian markets showed a mixed performance, with some indices rising while others remained flat or declined. The focus remains on economic indicators and upcoming central bank decisions. Investors are closely watching economic data and policy moves from President Donald Trump, as these factors are expected to influence global central bank decisions.
